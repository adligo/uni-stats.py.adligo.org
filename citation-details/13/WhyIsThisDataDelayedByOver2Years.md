Can you tell me why this website doesn't have data after 2023 for who is working by age, section 501.50?  https://nces.ed.gov/programs/digest/current_tables.asp


The National Center for Education Statistics (NCES) has data for **Table 501.50** (Employment-to-population ratios by age and education) up through **2023**, which is currently the "most current" available data for this specific metric.

Here is why you don't see 2024 or 2025 data yet:

### 1. Data Source Lag (Current Population Survey)

The data for Table 501.50 is derived from the **Current Population Survey (CPS)**, specifically the "Annual Social and Economic Supplement" conducted by the Census Bureau.

* **Reference Period:** NCES typically uses the **March** supplement of the CPS for these labor force tables.
* **Processing Time:** After the Census Bureau collects the raw data (e.g., March 2024), it must be cleaned and released by the Census Bureau, then analyzed and reformatted by NCES researchers into the specific educational categories you see in the Digest. This process usually results in a **12- to 18-month lag** between the data collection and its appearance in a Digest table.

### 2. Rolling Release Schedule

NCES no longer waits to release the entire "Digest of Education Statistics" as one giant book. Instead, they use a **rolling release schedule**.

* Table 501.50 was updated to include 2023 data in **mid-2024**.
* The **2024 data** is likely scheduled for release in **late spring or summer of 2025**.

### 3. Verification of "Most Current" Status

If you visit the [Most Current Digest Tables](https://nces.ed.gov/programs/digest/current_tables.asp) page, you are seeing the "Advance Release" versions.

* In the 2023 edition (which is the cycle currently being finalized), the latest year for this specific table is indeed 2023.
* You can verify this by looking at the table's footnote or title, which should read: *"Selected years, 1975 through 2023."*

### Where to find newer data (if you need it now)

If you need 2024 employment data specifically by age and education level before NCES publishes it, you can find it directly from the primary source:

* **Bureau of Labor Statistics (BLS):** Look for the "Employment Status of the Civilian Noninstitutional Population by Age, Sex, and Race" tables or the "Labor Force Statistics from the Current Population Survey."
* **Note:** The BLS publishes monthly and annual averages, but they may not match the NCES "Table 501.50" perfectly because NCES specifically filters and categorizes the data for educational research purposes.
